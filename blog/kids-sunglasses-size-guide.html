Kids Sunglasses Size Guide: Head Measurements from Baby to Teen

Wrong size is the most common reason kids sunglasses are returned — and the most common reason children refuse to wear them. A pair that slides down leaves UV gaps at the top. A pair that's too tight gets pulled off immediately. This guide gives you the exact measurements so you get it right first time.

How to Measure Your Child's Head Circumference

Head circumference is the single most reliable measurement for sizing children's sunglasses — more reliable than age alone, since head sizes vary significantly between children of the same age. The measurement takes under 60 seconds.

1

Get a soft tape measure (the kind used for sewing). A rigid ruler will not work.

2

Position 1cm above the eyebrows — across the forehead, above the ears.

3

Wrap around the widest part of the skull — this is usually just above the ears.

4

Note the measurement in centimetres where the tape overlaps at the forehead.

No tape measure? Use a strip of paper or string. Wrap it around the head, mark where it overlaps, then measure the marked length against a ruler.

What Happens with the Wrong Fit

Wrong-sized sunglasses don't just cause discomfort — they actively fail to protect the eye. Here's what goes wrong in each direction:

⚠ Too Big
  • Slides down the nose — UV enters from above the frame
  • Sits too far from face — UV gaps at the sides
  • Child removes them constantly (uncomfortable)
  • Heavy on nose bridge — pressure point pain
  • Gets returned — money and protection both wasted
✗ Too Small
  • Temples press the sides of the head — headaches
  • Frame too tight — leaves marks on face
  • Lens doesn't cover orbital area — UV from below
  • Child associates sunglasses with discomfort
  • Will be refused even when correctly sized later

The Perfect Fit Checklist

Once you've selected the right size range, use this checklist to confirm the specific pair fits correctly before committing to purchase.

  • ✓
    Temples lie flat without pressure. The arms should sit along the side of the head without pressing. No red marks after 10 minutes of wear.
  • ✓
    Nose bridge fits without sliding. The frame should not ride down the nose when looking downward. A small movement is acceptable; constant sliding is not.
  • ✓
    Lenses cover the orbital area. The lenses should extend beyond the eye socket — particularly above the brow and out to the temple. Hold the frame up to the light to verify no eye area is uncovered.
  • ✓
    Frame sits close to the face. There should be minimal gap between the frame edge and the face. A wide gap at the side means UV bypasses the lens entirely.
  • ✓
    Child moves freely without it falling. Have the child shake their head, bend forward, and look side to side. The frame should stay in position throughout.
  • ✓
    Weight feels comfortable. Lightweight frames under 20g are essential for small children who aren't used to wearing anything on their face.
