01 — Project Overview
Where you are.
Where you need to be.
Olivio & Co holds three Red Dot, iF, and D&AD awards and a product that genuinely outperforms the competition. The website does not reflect this. Customers land on a page that gives them no reason to trust, no guidance on sizing, no reviews to read, and no content to discover through search. This proposal covers a complete rebuild — three focused phases that close every gap between where the site is now and where an award-winning brand at this price point should be.

How we work

Every page is written and built from scratch — no edits to existing code. The current site stays live and untouched throughout. We hand over a fully tested, deployment-ready build at the end of Phase 3. Existing product data and catalog structure (database.js) is used as the source of truth — we do not rewrite individual product descriptions.

Phase Two · Weeks 5–8
Growth Engine
Everything the site needs to be found, read, and trusted by people who have never heard of Olivio & Co before.
SEO — Full Implementation
Every page title and meta description rewritten with the right keywords and the right intent. Structured data (schema markup) added for products, reviews, FAQ, and the brand itself — so Google can show star ratings and rich results in search. URL structure cleaned up (typos like "citrus-graden" corrected). Hreflang tags set correctly between .com and .eu to prevent duplicate content penalties. Sitemap and robots.txt reviewed and updated. Internal linking between collections and blog articles mapped and applied.
€ 1,800
Blog — Setup & Six Original Articles
The blog is currently empty. This is the single largest missed opportunity for organic traffic. We set up the blog properly (navigation link added, categories structured) and write six research-based articles targeting keywords that parents actually search for. Topics include: UV protection for children, how to choose the right size, screen time and blue light, ski goggle buying guide, and two additional articles based on keyword research at the time of writing. Each article is 600–900 words, SEO-structured, and written in plain English.
€ 2,400

A dedicated page answering the question every parent has: "Which size fits my child?" Six age categories, head circumference measurements in centimeters and inches, and a simple decision chart. The compounding effect

Each phase builds on the one before it. The homepage brings people in. The SEO and blog bring new people who would never have found the site otherwise. The review system, size guide, and trust elements turn browsers into buyers. None of these changes work in isolation — together, they shift the site from a brochure into a sales channel.

On social media content

Short-form video extends reach to audiences who will never find a website through search. When the content is eyewear-focused and genuinely watchable — not a product advertisement — it builds the kind of familiarity that turns a first-time visitor into a buyer without any friction. The 10-reel package covers approximately 10 weeks of consistent posting.
