Executive Summary

Strong product.
Weak conversion infrastructure.

OLIVIO & Co has the product, the design credentials, the sustainability story, and a market positioning — Mediterranean-inspired family eyewear with nine international design awards — that most competitors cannot replicate. The primary challenge is that none of these strengths are being communicated effectively within the purchase journey.

The fixes required are largely technical and marketing-operational, not product-related. A parent arriving on the homepage today sees no awards, no value proposition, a broken search bar — and on the Best Sellers carousel, Add to Cart buttons that also throw an error. A parent arriving on Babiators sees a guarantee badge, celebrity endorsements, and hundreds of star ratings. This gap is closable.

Brand Overview

The credentials
are world-class.

OLIVIO & Co was founded under Yellowdot Design and has accumulated nine international design awards across packaging, product, and sustainability categories. These are not participation trophies — Red Dot, iF Design, and ADC are the highest-profile awards in the global design industry.

  • 2020Red Dot AwardPackaging Design — Eco-Packaging

  • 2021iF Design AwardKids Sunglasses · Junior Sunglasses · Eco-Packaging
  • 2021D&AD AwardsShortlist — Packaging Design
  • 2022ADC 101st Annual AwardsBronze Cube + two Merit awards
  • 2022iF Design AwardScreen Sunglasses
  • 2022Green Product AwardBest of 2022 — Kids category
The problem

These awards are displayed as uncontextualised logos in a horizontal scroll strip on the homepage — no award name, no year, no category. A parent scrolling past sees nine logos they cannot identify and moves on. The award section on About Us is the only place these credentials are written out. They are never shown on product pages, near the cart, or in the checkout flow — which is where trust signals convert.

  • Mediterranean lifestyle — referenced in homepage hero, collection descriptions, and brand statement
  • Eco-conscious packaging — 100% biodegradable sugarcane pulp box, 100% recycled PET microfibre cloth; verified on product pages

  • Dual-market structure — olivioandco.com (USD, free shipping $59+) and olivioandco.eu (EUR, free shipping €69+), plus B2B wholesale at b2b.olivioandco.com

Navigation & Architecture

Three problems
in the first five seconds.

Before a visitor has seen a single product, they encounter a full-screen country modal, a broken search bar, and a homepage banner with a misspelled collection name. These are correctable in hours — and they are costing every visit.

Critical · First impression
Country selector modal blocks the entire homepage
Every visitor — regardless of country — is forced to choose between US and Europe before seeing any product. There is no automatic geolocation. This is a full-screen friction point before any brand impression is made. Shopify Markets with IP-based geolocation would eliminate it entirely.
Bug · Every page load
"Something went wrong. Please try again!" on search
The search bar displays an error message on page load — before the visitor has typed a single character. This is visible on every page of the site. A visitor who relies on search to navigate the catalogue encounters a broken experience immediately.
Typo · Homepage hero banner
"CREATIVE DEDITION" — misspelled in the hero carousel
The Creative Edition D collection is spelled "CREATIVE DEDITION" in the top homepage slider and its image alt text. The same collection is correctly spelled "CREATIVE EDITION D" in the lower Sunglasses Collections section on the same page. Two different spellings, same page.
Broken link · Revenue leak
Sport Sunglasses "Discover" CTA links to Amazon
The homepage Sport Sunglasses section has a "Discover" button that routes directly to an Amazon product listing. Every conversion from that click goes to Amazon — outside the brand's checkout, without customer data, without brand equity. One URL change fixes it.
SEO · URL slugs
Misspelled URLs: /sungalsses-oval, /sungalsses-round
Both navigation links and internal collection links use "sungalsses" (not "sunglasses") in the URL slug. Any external link to these URLs shares the typo. Redirecting to corrected slugs would recover any link equity already accumulated.
Missing · Navigation
Blog is footer-only — absent from main navigation
The blog at /blogs/news contains 40+ published articles and appears in the footer under About Us. It does not appear anywhere in the main navigation. The vast majority of visitors never discover it exists.
Inconsistency · Navigation
Breakfast Art "discover" links to /collections/citrus-graden
The Breakfast Art collection's discover button in the lower homepage section links to /collections/citrus-graden — a different collection entirely, and with another URL typo ("graden" instead of "garden"). A visitor clicking Breakfast Art lands on Citrus Garden.
Bug · Homepage
"Something went wrong" on Add to Cart in Best Sellers carousel
The Best Sellers product carousel on the homepage shows "Something went wrong! Please try again later." beneath every Add to Cart button. This is a separate bug from the search error — a visitor who finds a product they want on the homepage cannot add it to cart without clicking through to the product page first.
Broken link · Collection
Olivio Classic "discover" links to /collections/classicsunglasses
In the Sunglasses Collections section, the Olivio Classic "discover" button routes to /collections/classicsunglasses — a URL that does not match the correct /collections/classic slug used in the navigation menu. Visitors clicking it may land on a 404 or wrong page.
Positive
Navigation structure is logically organised
The mega-menu separates Sunglasses / Sport Glasses / Screen Glasses / Accessories clearly. The Shop by Age, Shop by Collection, and Shop by Style sub-groupings within Sunglasses are well-considered for a parental buyer navigating for a child's age group.

Homepage Analysis

What a parent
sees — and doesn't.

After clearing the country modal, a visitor lands on a full-width hero carousel showing collection names. No value proposition. No awards. No reviews. No guarantee. The strongest differentiators the brand has are all below the fold, buried on interior pages, or absent entirely.

What exists on the homepage

Content that is present

  • Hero carousel — Art of Breakfast / Classic Collection / Creative Edition D banners with Shop Now CTAs
  • Promotional strip — Silicone Accessories Set free with $89+ sunglasses orders (strong incentive, well positioned)
  • Mediterranean brand statement — "Inspired by the Mediterranean" with lifestyle imagery
  • Sunglasses Collections grid — Breakfast Art, Olivio Classic, Creative Edition D with editorial descriptions
  • Sport Glasses section — with the Amazon-linked Discover button
  • Best Sellers product carousel — 10+ products with prices, but Add to Cart buttons throw an error ("Something went wrong! Please try again later") on homepage — a separate bug from the search error
  • Awards section — 9 logo images in a horizontal scroll strip, no names or context
What is missing above the fold

What a first-time visitor never sees without scrolling

No tagline or value proposition explains what makes OLIVIO & Co different from any other children's eyewear brand. A new visitor has no immediate answer to "why this brand?" — only a collection name and a Shop Now button. Every competitor in the premium segment leads with their differentiation above the fold.

  • No customer reviews visible on the homepage — reviews do exist on product pages (confirmed: 5 reviews on Toddler Round Shark White, 9 reviews on #D Olive Green), but the homepage shows zero social proof; the most-trafficked page on the site has no peer validation
  • No award badges with context — Red Dot, iF Design, ADC logos appear far down the page with no names, years, or categories; they function as decoration rather than trust signals
  • No guarantee or warranty prominently visible — 13-month warranty and 30-day return window exist but are only visible as small icon strips at the bottom of individual product pages. Critically: returns are NOT free — the customer pays return shipping. This is a significant trust gap versus Babiators whose Lost & Found guarantee is unconditional.
  • No UV certification or safety badge — parents of young children make safety-first purchase decisions; CE mark and UV400 certification are not featured prominently
  • No email capture — no popup, no inline sign-up with incentive; the newsletter subscription exists only in the footer
  • No press or media mentions — if the brand has had coverage, it is not showcased

UV400, polarised lenses, BPA-free, eco-friendly packaging, and TR90 frame material are all present in product descriptions. The gap is structure: specs are listed in brief bullets rather than woven into searchable, conversion-oriented prose that connects the spec to the parent's concern.

Parents researching for children, and gift-buyers (grandparents, relatives), frequently save items before purchasing. Without a wishlist, this consideration intent is lost. For a product that is heavily gift-purchased, wishlists are a high-value retention tool.

Trust & Social Proof

The trust signals
exist. They're just invisible.

The brand's strongest trust assets — nine design awards, eco-certified packaging, a 13-month warranty — are all present but positioned where they don't convert. Awards are uncontextualised logos. The warranty icon strip is at the bottom of product pages. Reviews exist but in low volume and are invisible to Google. And the 30-day return window carries a catch: returns are not free.

What exists — verified

Trust infrastructure that is present

  • Nine design awards — Red Dot (2020), iF Design (2021, 2022), D&AD Shortlist (2021), ADC Bronze Cube (2022), Junior Design Awards Bronze (2021), Green Product Award Best of 2022 — all verified
  • Eco-sustainability credentials — biodegradable sugarcane pulp packaging, 100% recycled PET cleaning cloth, FSC-certified materials; present on product pages as spec bullets
  • 13-month warranty + 30-day return window — present as icon strips at the bottom of product pages; critically, returns are not free (customer pays return shipping, stated in FAQ) — this should be disclosed proactively rather than buried
  • Customer reviews — a review system is active and rendering on product pages; confirmed reviews on multiple products. Low volume overall, no photo reviews, not connected to Google via schema markup.
  • B2B wholesale portal — b2b.olivioandco.com signals trade credibility and distribution scale
  • Secure checkout — HTTPS confirmed; Shopify Payments with all major cards and PayPal
  • FAQ page — covers order tracking, modifications, promo codes, guest checkout, account management
What is missing

Critical gaps in the trust layer

  • Review volume is critically low — the system works, but most products have fewer than 10 reviews; no photo reviews; no star ratings in Google Search results due to missing AggregateRating schema; no reviews surfaced anywhere on the homepage
  • Award logos have no context — nine logos on the homepage with no award name, no year, no category; they are invisible as trust signals to a parent who does not recognise the logo
  • No UV certification badge displayed — CE mark, UV400, ANSI Z80.3 certifications are mentioned in text but not shown as verified certification icons; parents of young children specifically look for these
  • No Green Product Award badge on product pages — the 2022 eco award is not displayed at the point of purchase where it would influence eco-conscious parents
  • No press or media coverage showcased — if the brand has been featured in press, it is not visible to consumers
  • No UGC or customer photo gallery — no shoppable Instagram feed, no hashtag campaign gallery, no community content on the site

Content & Blog

Forty articles.
None of them about OLIVIO & Co.

The blog at olivioandco.com/blogs/news contains over 40 published articles across four pages. The volume is there. The brand specificity is not — every article reads as if it could have been published by any eyewear company in the world.

What exists

Blog content — confirmed article titles

Articles cover material science (TPEE vs. TR90 frame materials, lens materials), sunglasses history, UV protection, polarisation science, style guides (skin tone, hair colour), and trend roundups. Several directly address the target audience: "Are Sunglasses a Necessity for Kids?" and "Why Kids Should Wear Sunglasses" are relevant, keyword-aligned topics.

  • Positive: Topics like "kids sunglasses UV protection" and "TPEE vs TR90 for kids' frames" address genuine parental search intent
  • Problem 1: All 40+ articles appear to have been published in bulk in September 2024 — identical dates indicate a one-time content push. No new content published in 18+ months.
  • Problem 2: No buying guides ("Best Sunglasses for Toddlers 2025", "How to Choose Kids' Ski Goggles") — the highest-converting content format for this category
  • Problem 3: No seasonal content — summer eye protection, back-to-school screen glasses, ski season goggle guide; all three are major search windows this brand is uniquely positioned for
  • Problem 4: No internal linking from articles to product collection pages — blog content does not feed the purchase funnel
  • Problem 5: No content specific to OLIVIO & Co's collections, story, awards, or Mediterranean identity — the brand's differentiators do not appear in the blog
  • Problem 6: Some blog article URLs return 404 errors — content that was indexed by Google is now returning dead links, actively damaging SEO authority
The opportunity

Olivio & Co sells ski goggles for children. Neither Babiators nor Roshambo does. A single well-structured blog article targeting "kids ski goggles" published before October would compete in a keyword cluster with no direct competitor content. The same applies to blue-light / screen glasses for children, where both the category and the brand's product range are growing — with zero educational content currently on the site to capture that intent.

Customer Experience

Support infrastructure — what exists and what is missing

  • FAQ page covers order tracking, modifications, returns, promo codes, account management — adequate but the tone is blunt ("orders cannot be modified after submission") which can suppress confidence before checkout
  • EU accounts and US accounts are separate and not interoperable — explicitly noted in the FAQ; customers who switch regions or register on the wrong store lose their order history
  • No live chat widget confirmed on the US site
  • No loyalty rewards program — parents buying for growing children are a natural multi-year repeat customer base (toddler → kids → junior → teen); there is no incentive structure to retain them beyond the product itself
  • No wishlist in customer accounts — particularly high value for a gifting category
  • No "Find a Retailer" page — the B2B wholesale portal implies physical retail partners exist; consumers who prefer to buy in-store have no guidance
Sustainability — Verified claims

The eco story is real — it just needs a platform

OLIVIO & Co's sustainability credentials are externally verified and genuinely differentiated for the category. The Green Product Award 2022 in the Kids category is particularly strong — it is an independent assessment, not a self-claim. The problem is not the story; it is the positioning.

  • Packaging: 100% biodegradable sugarcane pulp moulded box — verified on product pages
  • Cleaning cloth: 100% recycled PET from reclaimed water bottles — verified on product pages
  • Green Product Award 2022 — Best of 2022, Kids category — externally validated
  • Brand commitment to continuous exploration of alternative sustainable materials — stated but not substantiated with specifics
  • Missing: No dedicated /pages/sustainability page; eco credentials are mentioned in product descriptions and About Us but have no standalone destination that can be shared, linked, or indexed
  • Missing: Green Product Award badge is not displayed on product pages or homepage where it would influence eco-conscious parents at the decision point
  • Missing: No third-party eco certification icons (FSC mark, recycled content symbol) alongside the relevant product specifications
