Frequently Asked Questions

Parents Ask, We Answer

Why do children need UV sunglasses?
Children's eyes transmit 70% more UV radiation than adult eyes, making UV400 protection essential from infancy. Cumulative UV exposure in childhood is a leading risk factor for cataracts and macular degeneration later in life. Sunglasses for kids are not a fashion accessory — they are essential eye protection, especially at southern latitudes and near reflective surfaces like water or snow.
What age should kids start wearing sunglasses?
From birth. Olivio & Co covers 6 age categories: Baby (0–12m), Toddler (1–3y), Kids (3–6y), Junior (6–10y), Junior+ (10–12y), Teen & Adult (12+). Each category is precisely sized for head circumference to ensure a safe, comfortable fit that stays on without pressure points. Use the size guide above to find the right category.
What size sunglasses does my child need?
Measure around the widest part of your child's head above the ears:

Baby (0–12m): 40–44 cm  |  Toddler (1–3y): 44–48 cm  |  Kids (3–6y): 48–51 cm  |  Junior (6–10y): 51–54 cm  |  Junior+ (10–12y): 54–56 cm  |  Teen & Adult (12+): 56+ cm.

When between sizes, we recommend sizing up for comfort.
Do kids need ski goggles — or can they wear sunglasses on the slopes?
Ski goggles are strongly recommended for skiing and snowboarding. UV radiation is 40% stronger at altitude, and reflected snow doubles UV exposure. Beyond UV, goggles provide full-face wind protection and are designed to stay fixed even during falls.

Olivio & Co junior ski goggles include UV400 anti-fog lenses — and we are the only major kids eyewear brand with a dedicated ski goggles category. Neither Babiators nor Roshambo offers this.
