Part One — Current State

Where the site
stands today.

Olivio & Co has two stores — olivioandco.eu and olivioandco.com — a 5-year content archive, an Instagram account with over 5,500 followers, and a product range that includes a category (ski goggles) neither of its two main competitors offers. On paper, this is a strong SEO position to build from.

In practice, almost none of it is working for search. The schema is broken, analytics is absent, and the site's strongest differentiators are invisible to Google. The blog exists — over 40 articles across four pages — but its content is generic industry copy that could belong to any eyewear brand. None of it earns rankings for Olivio & Co specifically. What follows is a precise account of what is missing, what is broken, and what is sitting unused.

Missing
No analytics installed
There is currently no tracking tool on either store. Every visit, every product view, every abandoned cart is invisible. There is no baseline to measure against.
Missing
Star ratings absent from Google Search
AggregateRating schema is not present. Both Babiators and Roshambo show star ratings in their search listings. Olivio & Co does not.
Missing
Organisation + WebSite schema absent
No brand logo, social profiles, or contact info in structured data. No Sitelinks Search Box. These are the foundational schema blocks Google uses to understand what the brand is.
Misdirected
Blog exists — but the content is generic
The site has over 40 published articles across four blog pages — "When Were Sunglasses First Invented?", "Sunglasses Trends 2024", "Selecting the Perfect Sunglass Lens Material." None of it is specific to Olivio & Co, its brand, its collections, or its uncontested categories. A competitor could publish this content word-for-word. The blog link exists in the footer but is absent from the main navigation — most visitors never find it.
Buried
Guarantee policy exists — but invisible above the fold
A 13-month warranty and 30-day return policy are present on product pages as small icon strips at the bottom. For both main competitors, this guarantee is the homepage's primary trust signal — displayed prominently before a visitor even scrolls. On Olivio & Co, a parent browsing the homepage never sees it.
Missing
No size or fit guide
Five age categories — Toddler 0–2y, Kids 2–4y, Junior 3–7y, Junior+ 7–10y, Teen & Adult — with no supporting size guide. There is also an overlap between Kids (2–4y) and Junior (3–7y) that a parent trying to choose will find confusing. Uncertainty about fit is one of the most common reasons parents do not complete a children's eyewear purchase.
Broken
Search bar errors on every page load
The site search function throws an error before the visitor types a single character. A visitor who lands from any page and uses search immediately encounters a broken experience.
Inconsistent
Product descriptions present but not keyword-structured
Product pages include UV400, polarised, eco-packaging and collection story details — better than the original audit suggested. The gap is in keyword structure and length: specs are listed as bullet points rather than woven into searchable prose. No head circumference range, no age guidance in-copy, no link to a size guide.
Inactive
Google Business Profile exists but unused
The profile is live but dormant. No posts, no Q&A, no photo updates. Both competitors maintain active profiles. GBP activity is a confirmed factor in Maps visibility.
Present but buried
Nine awards on the homepage — but not working as trust signals
Nine design award logos are displayed on the homepage in a horizontal scroll strip. They are present, but they carry no context — no award name, no year, no what-it-means. A parent scrolling past sees logos they don't recognise and moves on.
Broken copy
"Creative Dedition" — a typo on the homepage
The Creative Edition D collection is misspelled as "CREATIVE DEDITION" in the homepage banner. This is the brand's most distinctive product line, named prominently. A typo at this level signals careless execution to a discerning buyer.
Channel conflict
Sport sunglasses "Discover" button links to Amazon, not the store
The homepage Sport Sunglasses section has a "Discover" CTA that routes traffic directly to Amazon — outside the brand's own checkout. This leaks revenue, undermines SEO authority, and hands customer data to a third party. Visitors drawn in by the brand end up buying on Amazon.

Competitor Analysis

Where the market
stands, and where the gaps are.

Two competitors were analysed in depth: Babiators (US) and Roshambo Eyewear (Italy/US). Both are direct category competitors with established SEO presence.

Babiators — babiators.com 🇺🇸

Strengths to be aware of

Babiators built their brand on a free replacement guarantee — lost or broken, replaced, no questions asked. This single policy, displayed prominently on every page, drives conversion more than any other element. They supplement it with celebrity social proof (Kardashian kids, Justin Timberlake's son), an Eco Collection made from ocean-bound plastic, and a Screen Savers range targeting parental screen-time anxiety. They are Forbes Top 100.

  • Free 1-year replacement guarantee — #1 homepage trust signal
  • Screen Savers (blue light lenses) — addresses the fastest-growing parental concern
  • No Europe-specific store — Olivio & Co's .eu domain has a structural advantage
  • No ski goggles — this entire category is unclaimed by Babiators
  • No artistic or cultural brand identity — purely functional positioning
  • Very limited brand-specific content — Olivio & Co has 40+ blog articles but all are generic industry copy; brand-driven SEO territory remains open
Roshambo Eyewear — roshambo.com 🇮🇹🇺🇸

The more sophisticated competitor

Roshambo competes on depth: Made in Italy premium perception, prescription kids glasses (a major differentiator with almost no competition), a free home try-on kit, charity partnerships (autism, Down syndrome, childhood cancer), and a genuinely comprehensive blog covering UV guides, age-by-age sizing, and parenting content. Their blog alone generates meaningful organic traffic that Olivio & Co currently does not compete for at all.

  • Free Home Try-On Kit — removes the primary e-commerce friction point
  • Prescription kids glasses — unique in the market, no competitor matches this
  • 60-day returns, broken/lost/scratched replacement — more generous than Babiators
  • No Europe-specific experience — Olivio & Co's most durable structural advantage
  • No ski goggles — same gap as Babiators; winter category entirely uncontested
  • No Mediterranean or artistic brand identity — functional colour palette only

Application — Technical Foundation

The infrastructure
that makes everything else work.

Before content, before keywords, before any campaign — these are the technical prerequisites. Without them, every other improvement is undermined.

01
Analytics

Install Google Analytics 4 with full conversion tracking.

The site currently has no measurement tool installed. From day one of the new site, every visit, traffic source, product view, cart addition, and completed purchase is tracked. This is not optional — it is the baseline that makes every subsequent decision evidence-based rather than guesswork. Conversion events are configured from the first session: add-to-cart, checkout-initiated, purchase-completed.

  • GA4 property connected to both EU and US stores with cross-domain tracking configured
  • Google Search Console connected to GA4 — keyword impressions and click data flow into one dashboard
  • Conversion events set up and verified against real test purchases before launch
  • Baseline report generated from first 30 days — traffic by source, conversion rate by collection, top exit pages
02
Technical Fixes

Fix search, correct redirects, repair every broken URL.

The search bar error is corrected at the theme level. Every URL that was previously incorrect is identified, corrected in the new site, and redirected — any link that existed to an old URL, whether from Google's index, a blog post, or an external site, lands correctly on the new URL without showing a 404. No existing search equity is lost in the migration.

  • Amazon CTA fix — Sport sunglasses "Discover" button currently routes to Amazon. Redirected to /collections/sport-glasses to keep traffic, revenue, and customer data within the brand's own store
  • "Creative Dedition" typo — corrected to "Creative Edition D" across homepage banners and all collection references
  • Search function repaired and tested across all device and browser combinations
  • 301 redirects mapped for every changed URL — old → new, confirmed working before launch
  • Sitemap regenerated and submitted to Google Search Console on launch day
  • Core Web Vitals — image optimisation and script loading reviewed for performance improvements
03
Schema — Organisation + WebSite

Tell Google what this brand is.

Two JSON-LD schema blocks are added to the site's theme header. The Organization block establishes the brand identity in Google's knowledge graph — logo URL, social profiles, customer service contact, available languages. The WebSite + SearchAction block enables the Sitelinks Search Box on branded queries, so visitors searching 'Olivio & Co' on Google can search the store directly from results without a separate click.

  • Organization schema — name, URL, logo, sameAs (Instagram + Facebook), contactPoint with language support
  • WebSite schema — name, URL, potentialAction with SearchAction and urlTemplate for Shopify search
  • Validation — both blocks tested in Google Rich Results Test and schema.org validator before deployment

Application — Schema & Reviews

Star ratings in Google
Search — and how to get them.

04
Schema — AggregateRating (Star Ratings)

Install a review app, collect reviews, activate schema.

Shopify does not generate AggregateRating schema automatically. A review app is required — it both collects reviews and injects the correct schema markup on each product page. Once at least one review per product is indexed, star ratings begin appearing in Google Search results within 2–4 weeks. The click-through rate improvement from star ratings in search is consistent across benchmarks at approximately 30%.

  • Judge.me (recommended — free plan, excellent schema, automated post-purchase review requests)
  • Post-purchase email sequence configured to request a review 7–10 days after delivery — this is how the review base is built
  • Review widgets placed on product listings and homepage — visible social proof at every decision point
  • Schema validation — Rich Results Test run after first reviews are collected to confirm star schema is being read
05
Schema — FAQPage + Shipping

Expand search result real estate with FAQ and shipping schema.

FAQPage schema is added to the /pages/faqs URL. Correctly implemented, this causes the FAQ content to expand directly in Google Search results — the listing occupies 2–5× more SERP space, with each question and answer visible without a click. Additionally, ShippingDetails schema is added to product pages to surface the '€69 free shipping' threshold in Google Shopping results.

  • FAQPage JSON-LD written for each Q&A pair on the FAQ page, validated before deployment
  • Recommended FAQ additions — sizing guide, returns policy, UV standard, shipping details — all high-intent pre-purchase questions
  • ShippingDetails schema — free shipping threshold (€69), shipping zones, and estimated delivery times added to product schema
  • ItemList schema on collection pages — allows individual products to appear as list items in SERP
Schema validation tools — use after every change

All schema changes must be validated in Google Rich Results Test (search.google.com/test/rich-results) and schema.org Validator before going live. After deployment, monitor Google Search Console → Enhancements → Rich Results for indexing status and any errors. Never deploy schema changes without validating first — malformed markup can suppress existing rich results.

Application — On-Page SEO

Every product page
rewritten for search.

06
Product Page Copy

Rewrite every product description to 150+ words, structured for search.

Product pages include UV400, polarised, BPA-free and eco-packaging details — the raw material is there. The gap is in structure: specs are listed as brief bullet points rather than woven into searchable prose that Google can index as meaningful content. No head circumference range, no age guidance in the description body, no link to a size guide, no mention of the collection story beyond a title. Rewriting every product description as structured prose is the single highest-impact SEO action on existing content — it improves rankings without requiring new pages or external links.

  • Minimum 150 words per product — UV400 spec, polarisation, BPA-free material, age range, head circumference guide reference
  • Collection story woven in — Creative D, Coral Reef, Breakfast Art — the artistic context that differentiates from generic descriptions
  • Primary keyword in first sentence — e.g. 'toddler sunglasses UV protection' or 'junior ski goggles' — natural, not forced
  • Returns and guarantee integrated into each product description — a conversion element as much as an SEO element
07
Collection Pages + Meta Data

Unique meta titles and descriptions for every collection URL.

Collection pages are among the highest-traffic entry points from organic search — they rank for category queries ('toddler sunglasses EU', 'kids ski goggles buy') rather than brand queries. Currently, collection pages have generic or duplicated meta data. Each collection receives a unique, keyword-optimised title tag and meta description written specifically for its search cluster.

  • Title tag structure: Primary keyword + Brand Name — e.g. 'Toddler Sunglasses UV400 | Olivio & Co'
  • Meta descriptions: 150–160 characters, written to earn the click — include the unique differentiator (EU shipping, awards, Mediterranean)
  • H1 tags — each collection page gets a single, descriptive H1 that matches the primary keyword target
  • Keyword integration — top 20 keywords from this report distributed across collection pages based on intent match
08
Size Guide Page

Build the definitive children's sunglasses size guide.

A dedicated /pages/size-guide page is created. It covers all five age categories with age ranges, head circumference measurements in cm and inches, and a comparison table that clarifies the 3–4 year overlap between Kids and Junior. This page serves two purposes simultaneously: it reduces cart abandonment from parents uncertain about fit, and it ranks for the 'children's sunglasses size' search cluster that Olivio & Co's granular age system is uniquely positioned to own.

  • Age range table — Toddler (0–2y), Kids (2–4y), Junior (3–7y), Junior+ (7–10y), Teen & Adult — with explicit head circumference ranges in cm and inches
  • Head circumference guide — in cm and inches, with measuring instructions
  • Linked from every product page — visible next to the size selector, not buried in navigation
  • SEO target: 'kids sunglasses size guide', 'what size sunglasses for 3 year old', 'toddler junior sunglasses fit'

Blog Restructure + Navigation

The newsletter list is a channel Olivio & Co owns entirely — no algorithm, no ad spend, no competitor can take it away.

  • Email capture — exit-intent popup with first-order incentive, targeting visitors who have browsed but not purchased
